#Urban Economics and Land Markets
Research on how urban land and property markets function, including land values, housing prices, rents, development timing, agglomeration effects, accessibility, market segmentation, and the interaction between regulation and market outcomes. I am especially interested in work that explains how prices, land use, and development behaviour respond to infrastructure, zoning, subdivision, transport improvements, and local amenity change. Papers are most relevant when they combine conceptual urban economics with empirical analysis that can inform real-world planning and policy.

#Housing Supply, Property Markets, and Development Feasibility
Research on housing supply, residential development, property market dynamics, absorption, construction costs, development feasibility, and the economics of real estate projects. I am particularly interested in papers that distinguish demand-side pressure from supply-side constraint, and that examine how regulation, infrastructure capacity, finance, and landowner behaviour affect the pace, form, and viability of development. Work is especially useful where it informs forecasting, scenario analysis, or feasibility assessment in urban contexts.

#Spatial Equilibrium and Urban Simulation Modelling
Research on spatial equilibrium models, urban simulation, land use allocation, residential sorting, gravity models, and integrated urban-economic modelling frameworks. I am especially interested in methods that link households, employment, accessibility, land values, housing supply, and planning constraints across space. Papers are most relevant where they offer practical modelling approaches, calibration strategies, or empirical tests that can support scenario planning and policy analysis, particularly in cities with fragmented markets and uneven infrastructure.

#Land Value Capture, Infrastructure, and Municipal Finance
Research on land value capture, development charges, betterment levies, infrastructure finance, cost recovery, fiscal incidence, and the distribution of land uplift created by public action. I am particularly interested in papers that examine how infrastructure provision, regulatory change, and coordinated public investment affect land values and development outcomes, and how municipalities can recover part of that value in efficient and equitable ways. Work is most relevant where it engages with implementation challenges in fast-growing or institutionally constrained urban settings.

#Urban Planning, Regulation, and Growth Management
Research on planning systems, zoning, rezoning, subdivision, development rights, infrastructure bottlenecks, and the governance of urban growth. I am especially interested in work that examines coordination failures, political economy, landowner opposition, development uncertainty, and other constraints on denser or more efficient urban development. Papers are most useful when they connect institutional processes to measurable effects on supply, prices, land use, and infrastructure demand.

#Evaluation of Public Safety and Policing Technologies
Research on the effectiveness, efficiency, and broader impacts of policing and public safety technologies, including CCTV, acoustic gunshot detection, drones, aerial surveillance, and related digital monitoring systems. I am particularly interested in rigorous causal evaluation using quasi-experimental methods, spatial analysis, natural experiments, synthetic control, event studies, and related approaches. Work is most relevant where it examines crime outcomes, operational efficiency, resource deployment, displacement, spillovers, implementation conditions, and cost effectiveness.

#Remote Sensing, Geospatial Data, and Building Detection
Research on the use of remote sensing, satellite imagery, aerial imagery, machine learning, and geospatial analysis for identifying buildings, measuring land use change, tracking settlement growth, and deriving urban indicators. I am especially interested in work using building footprint data, population surfaces, Earth observation products, image classification, object detection, and validation methods relevant to urban and regional analysis. Papers are most useful when they deal with practical issues of accuracy, transferability, and application in data-scarce settings.

#Urban and Regional Growth Modelling in Applied Planning
Research on land use growth modelling, spatial development scenarios, regional forecasting, accessibility change, infrastructure-led growth, and practical modelling approaches used in planning and policy. I am particularly interested in models that support applied decision-making, including municipal growth management, nodal planning, infrastructure sequencing, and the translation of spatial data into operational planning tools. Work is especially relevant where it bridges academic rigour and implementable modelling practice.

#Cost-Benefit Analysis, Infrastructure Economics, and Energy Systems
Research on cost-benefit analysis, project appraisal, infrastructure economics, energy security, electricity generation, and the economic evaluation of public and private investment. I am particularly interested in methods for valuing reliability, resilience, system costs, and wider economic impacts in cases such as peaking generation, open cycle gas turbines, and infrastructure with high strategic value. Papers are most relevant where they deal with appraisal under uncertainty, scenario comparison, or difficult-to-measure public benefits.

#Freight Logistics, Road-to-Rail Transition, and Agri-Logistics
Research on freight transport, logistics systems, modal shift from road to rail, corridor efficiency, agricultural value chains, and the economics of moving fresh produce and other time-sensitive goods. I am especially interested in papers that examine generalised cost, reliability, cold chain requirements, terminal and intermodal systems, spatial production networks, and the institutional barriers to rail-based logistics. Work is most relevant when it can inform feasibility analysis, corridor planning, and the comparative economics of logistics alternatives.

#Archaeology of the Mediterranean
Research on Mediterranean archaeology, with particular interest in settlement systems, colonisation, trade, landscape archaeology, material culture, connectivity, and long-term regional change. I am especially interested in work that combines archaeology with geography, environmental history, or spatial analysis, and in studies that illuminate how landscapes, ports, routes, and urban centres evolved over time. Papers are most relevant when they are analytically grounded rather than purely descriptive.

#History and Archaeology of Sicily
Research on the history and archaeology of Sicily across the ancient, medieval, early modern, and modern periods. I am particularly interested in Greek colonisation, indigenous societies, Roman Sicily, late antiquity, Arab-Norman Sicily, historical geography, military history, and the relationship between landscape, settlement, and political change. Work is most relevant where it helps interpret places, routes, monuments, or regions in Sicily in a deeper historical context, especially with strong use of evidence, maps, material remains, or historiographical debate.
